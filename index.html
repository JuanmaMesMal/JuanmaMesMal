Biography

  • +
    2003

    Saiko, born Miguel Cantos Gómez in Granada, Spain, began his musical journey alongside his brother in a duo called Wido & Saiko. From those early studio sessions in his hometown, his passion for music and storytelling quickly became evident.

  • +
    2020

    In 2020, he started his solo career, crafting a distinctive urban sound that blends heartfelt melodies with raw emotion. His breakout came with songs like “Polaris”.

  • +
    2023

    In 2023, hits like “Supernova” and “Buenas” (with Quevedo) topped the charts, confirming his place among Spain’s leading urban artists.

  • +
    2024

    By 2024, Saiko released his first studio album, “Sakura”, a project that reflected his maturity, versatility, and emotional depth. That same year, he achieved another milestone by becoming the main sponsor of Granada CF, proudly representing his city both in music and in sport.

  • +
    2025

    By 2025, Saiko continued to evolve artistically with the release of Natsukashii Yoru, an album whose Japanese title means “nostalgic night.” This project showcases a more intimate and reflective side of the artist, blending urban rhythms with emotional storytelling. Through thirteen tracks, Saiko opens up about love, growth, and the passage of time, using nostalgia not as sadness but as a way to celebrate memories. With Natsukashii Yoru, he explores new sounds that merge trap, pop, and electronic textures, offering a cinematic journey that reflects his maturity and versatility. The album also highlights his ability to balance introspective lyrics with infectious melodies, proving that his artistry goes far beyond commercial success. This release solidified Saiko’s position as one of the most creative voices in the new wave of Spanish music, connecting deeply with his audience both in emotion and authenticity.
